1) Decide whether the LLC will have a single or multiple members: this will affect the tax structure and financial reporting requirements.
2) Ensure your business name is unique in Wyoming and does not infringe on anyone else's trademark: https://wyobiz.wyo.gov/business/filingsearch.aspx
3) Prepare and file Articles of Organization via Wyoming Online Business Filing System: https://wyobiz.wyo.gov/Business/RegistrationType.aspx
4) Starting in 2024, the Corporate Transparency Act applies in the U.S., requiring the submission of Beneficial Ownership Information (BOI) to Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN).
It shoud be done in 30 days.
Regulatory framework: 31 CFR § 1010.380, https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/31/1010.380
For late submission or failure to submit the report, fines are provided up to $500 per day, as well as possible criminal liability: 31 U.S.C. § 5336(h), https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/5336.
5) Draft the Operating Agreement: it regulates the rights and obligations of participants, the distribution of profits, and the management structure of the LLC.
In Wyoming, this document is not always required by law, but it is recommended to protect the business and clearly delineate responsibilities, especially if there are multiple participants.
6) Obtain an Employer Identification Number (EIN) from the IRS for tax accounting via the IRS website: https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/get-an-employer-identification-number
7) Open a business account in an U.S. bank.
It can be done online via Mercury, Wise, Relay Financial, BlueVine.
8) Analyse the dietary supplements selling regulations:
8.1) FDA (Food and Drug Administration) requirements
8.2) The Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act (DSHEA)
8.3) Labeling Requirements
8.4) «Current good manufacturing practice in manufacturing, packaging, labeling, or holding operations for dietary supplements» (cGMP).
21 CFR Part 111: https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/part-111
8.5) Regulation in Wyoming.
9) Because your manufacturing is outside the U.S., it is necessary to register with the FDA each «food facility» where the product is manufactured .
10) Compliance with FSMA (Food Safety Modernization Act) is required.
11) An LLC can be taxed as a «pass-through entity» (profits and losses are reported on the participants' returns) or as a corporation if the corresponding classification is chosen.
12) Obtain Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN).
It is necessary to report income in the U.S. (for example, by filing Form 1040-NR under «pass-through» taxation).
26 CFR § 301.6109-1: https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/26/301.6109-1
13) If an LLC has a single foreign participant (and is treated as a «disregarded entity» for tax purposes), it is necessary to file Form 5472 with the IRS annually: https://www.irs.gov/forms-pubs/about-form-5472
Penalties for failure to file or late filing start at $25K.
Form 5472 must be submitted together with Form 1120.
14) When selling goods to other U.S. states (for example, California, Texas, etc.), a tax nexus may arise in those states.
In such a case, it is necessary:
14.1) Determine in which states the «economic nexus» thresholds are exceeded.
14.2) Register for the collection and remittance of Sales Tax in those states where the obligation arises.
14.3) Collect and remit the tax to the budget of the respective state through its tax portal (reporting frequency is monthly or quarterly, depending on the state's rules).
15) In Australia, it is necessary to declare foreign income, taking into account the provisions of the «Double Taxation Taxes on Income Convention Between the United States of America and Australia» (https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-trty/aus.pdf
16) In Wyoming, it is necessary to file an Annual Report and pay the corresponding fee annually.
This is done online: https://wyobiz.wyo.gov/Business/ARWizard.aspx
17) Set up an accounting system to record the company's financial transactions.
18) Analyze the requirements of customs authorities in the U.S. and Australia concerning your product:
18.1) The FDA requires «Prior Notice» when importing food products (including dietary supplements) into the territory of the U.S..
18.2) Customs and Border Protection may request additional documentation confirming the product's compliance with FDA requirements.
19) When selling dietary supplements in the U.S., the brand owner and the importer bear responsibility for the product's safety.
Product Liability Insurance is recommended to reduce financial risks.
20) In a number of states (Massachusetts, Michigan, New Jersey, etc.), prescription drugs are completely exempt from sales tax.
21) In a number of states (Oregon, Delaware, Montana, etc.). there are no sales tax at all. 
22) The basic sales tax rate in Wyoming is 4% (plus possible local additions of 1-2%).
